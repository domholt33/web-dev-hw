Skate Boarding How To: The Ollie

  The ollie is the fundamental basis for all flat ground tricks on a skateboard. It is used as a technique that allows you to jump into the air while the board follows your feet. Before you can learn some of the more exciting looking tricks, such as the kickflip, you must first understand how to properly ollie. Follow the steps in this instructable to better understand the process for learning and performing the ollie.


  It is important to understand safety and differences in skill levels while learning to skateboard. There are inherent safety risks when skateboarding that can be avoided if necessary percautions are taken. Please reference the graph below to determine where you should begin learning how to ollie.

Learning Surfaces
Beginner(first time on a skateboard) Stationary on carpet
Intermediate(can balance,stand, and roll on a skateboard on hard surfaces) Stationary on wood or concrete
Experienced(very comfortable with rolling on hard surcaces and can ollie while stationary) Concrete or wood while rolling


What you will need

  1. Skateboard
  2. Skateboarding Shoes
  3. Flat Surface (concrete, carpet, plywood, etc..)
  4. Helmet
  5. Knee, Elbow, Wrist pads(optional)

Step One: Foot Position



Place your back foot high up and in the center of the tail of the board while your front foot is placed just behind the bolts with your toes at the edge of the board. Bend at the knees as if you are going to jump.


Step Two



Jump into the air and as you do, violently push the tail of the board into the ground to create the pop of the board off of the ground.


Step Three



Slide your front foot upwards and forwards towards the nose of the board creating friction between your front foot and the grip tape.


Step Four



Lift your back foot up to allow the tail of the board to rise and once your feet are evenly tucked you can prepare for the landing.


Step Five



Land with your knees bent to absorb the shock, then stand up and ride away from your successful ollie!
